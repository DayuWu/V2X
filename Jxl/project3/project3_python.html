Background¶

With the popularization of smart phones and the rapid increase of mobile phone users, shared bicycles, as an important part of the urban transportation system, are booming with the characteristics of green, environmental protection, convenience and efficiency, economy and environmental protection. By providing services on campuses, bus stations, residential areas, public service areas, etc., bicycle-sharing companies complete the last puzzle of the transportation industry and create synergy with other public transportation methods. Shared bicycles can help alleviate urban short-distance traffic travel and the last mile problem. However, due to its operational characteristics, shared bicycles pose greater challenges for companies in the planning and management of city deployment and dispatch of bicycles.

Besides, among the data given by the teacher, we have selected data about the Internet of Vehicles. Our interest lies in studying the driving behavior of people or the relationship between humans and the geographic world.

In particular, I am very interested in getting the information contained in the latitude and longitude data. However, the latitude and longitude data in the data given by the teacher is too small, and the data collection point is relatively remote, so I used the data from the https://www.heywhale.com/home website. The data is based on Shanghai Mobike's random sampling of about 100,000 open order data in August 2016 for analysis, to dig out the laws behind the data, and use the data to outline the current situation of the use of Mobike shared bicycles and user travel.In the meanwhile, We analyze the number of Mobike bicycles used to help the company rationally arrange the number of parking spots.

# Calculate the distance of the ride by the latitude and longitude 
# of the starting point and the end of the ride, the unit is kilometers

def bike_distance(item):
    longitude_start, latitude_start, longitude_end, latitude_end = map(radians, [item["start_location_x"], item["start_location_y"], item["end_location_x"], item["end_location_y"]]) # 
    dist_longitude = longitude_start - longitude_end
    dist_latitude = latitude_start - latitude_end
    
    dist = sin(dist_latitude/2)**2 + cos(latitude_start) * cos(latitude_end) * sin(dist_longitude/2)**2
    
    distance = round(2 * asin(sqrt(dist)) * 6371,3)  
    return distance

data_bike["dist"] = data_bike.apply(bike_distance,axis=1)

# Obtain the route through the trail of each bike 

def geo_Length(item):
    track_list = item["track"].split("#")  
#     print(track_list)
    
    part_Length_item = {}
    global Length 
    Length = 0
    
    for i in range(len(track_list)-1):
        
        start_location = track_list[i].split(",")
        end_location = track_list[i+1].split(",")
        
        part_Length_item["start_location_x"],part_Length_item["start_location_y"] = float(start_location[0]),float(start_location[1])
        part_Length_item["end_location_x"],part_Length_item["end_location_y"] = float(end_location[0]),float(end_location[1])
        
        # distance
        part_length_each = bike_distance(part_Length_item)
        Length = part_length_each + Length
        
    return Length


data_bike["Length"] = data_bike.apply(geo_Length,axis=1)

From the chart, we observe that the data has an obvious long tail, showing a right skewed distribution .

We know it is because the behavior of people using shared bicycles is likely to travel for short periods of time. The data range on the x-axis is very large, and this result is partly due to abnormal data records.

From the data range of the x-axis of the chart, we observe that there is a riding time as long as 800 mins . Its obviously abnormal to ride continuously for more than 800 mins. Such data records may be caused by the user forgetting to turn off the lock during riding.

For the abnormal riding time data, we add a new column of "speed", and by eliminating the obviously abnormal data of the riding speed. Because the riding speed is generally 12- 20km/h, we can remove the abnormal record caused by the user forgetting to close the lock after riding.

From the image, we conclude that the travel time of shared bicycles is mostly between 20min and 30min.

def district_diff(item):
    item["start_location_x"] = round(item["start_location_x"],2)
    item["start_location_y"] = round(item["start_location_y"],2)
    item["end_location_x"] = round(item["end_location_x"],2)
    item["end_location_y"] = round(item["end_location_y"],2)
    
    return item

    response = requests.get(base, parameters)
